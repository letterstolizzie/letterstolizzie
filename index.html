The Year of Magical Thinking

Joan Didion

A memoir about loss so raw it reads like a wound. Didion writes grief not as a phase but as a place you live in, where logic stops working and love refuses to believe what happened.

why it feels like lizzie

grief that doesn't ask permission to stay. she would underline every page. she would recognize the way loss rewrites ordinary moments into something unbearable.
